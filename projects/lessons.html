Lesson 1: Greetings in Asante Twi; A Key to Connection

Greetings play an essential role in Asante Twi, reflecting the culture’s emphasis on respect, warmth, and community. In Ghanaian society, greeting someone properly sets the tone for a positive interaction and is a vital part of daily communication.

Basic Greetings in Asante Twi

English Asante Twi
Good Morning Maakye
Good Afternoon Maaha
Good Evening Maadwo
Good Night Da yie
How are you? Wo ho te sen?
I'm fine, thank you Me ho yɛ, medaase
Goodbye Nante yie

Alphabet

The Asante Twi alphabet consists of 22 letters. It includes both vowels and consonants, with no letters C, J, Q, V, X, and Z.

Category Letters
Vowels A, E, Ɛ, I, O, Ɔ, U
Consonants B, D, F, G, H, K, L, M, N, P, R, S, T, W, Y

Writing System

Twi is a tonal language, meaning pitch affects meaning. It uses Latin script, and the vowels Ɛ and Ɔ are distinct from E and O in pronunciation.

  • Tonal Nature: The pitch used can change the meaning of a word.
  • Pronunciation: The vowels Ɛ and Ɔ are different from E and O.
  • Spelling Structure: Words are written phonetically to match pronunciation.
